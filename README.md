<h3 dir="ltr">Understanding Pot Odds and Expected Value</h3>
<p dir="ltr">Pot odds and expected value (EV) are critical concepts that every serious player must grasp.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Pot odds represent the ratio between the current size of the pot and the cost of a contemplated call. By calculating pot odds, you can make informed decisions about whether to continue investing in a hand. For example, if the pot is $100 and the cost to call is $20, the pot odds are 5:1. If your chances of winning exceed those odds, calling may be justified.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Expected value, on the other hand, assesses how profitable a bet or call can be over the long run. Understanding these concepts helps players evaluate risk versus reward, ensuring that your decisions lead toward consistent profitability over time.</p>
